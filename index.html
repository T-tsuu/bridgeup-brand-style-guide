Iconography

Style rules, sizing, color usage, and in-context examples for all Bridge Up UI icons.

01 — Icon Style Rules

✓ Stroke
✗ Filled
Stroke style only All UI icons use a consistent outlined stroke style — never solid filled. This keeps them light, modern, and aligned with the brand's clean aesthetic. Stroke width: 1.8px at 24px · scale proportionally at other sizes
✓ Round
✗ Square
Rounded line caps & joins All stroke ends and joins must use stroke-linecap="round" and stroke-linejoin="round". This matches the rounded, approachable character of Montserrat and the pill-shaped buttons. Never use: square caps, miter joins, or butt ends
24×24px design grid All icons are designed on a 24×24px grid with 2px padding on each side, giving an effective drawing area of 20×20px. This ensures consistent optical weight across the entire icon set. Grid: 24×24px · Padding: 2px · Draw area: 20×20px
Lucide
Recommended icon library Lucide Icons is the primary icon library for Bridge Up. It is open-source, uses a consistent stroke style with rounded caps, and has a 24px grid — a perfect match for this brand system. Use it for all UI icons before drawing custom ones. Library: Lucide Icons (lucide.dev) · License: ISC (free, commercial use) · Format: SVG / React / Vue components available

05

Imagery & Photography

Rules for selecting, treating, and using photos and illustrations across all touchpoints.

01 — Photography Principles

Real people, real moments Show authentic interactions — a student reviewing feedback on a laptop, a recruiter in a short video call, a university coordinator looking at data. Avoid staged, overly polished stock poses.
Algerian & diverse context Imagery should reflect the Algerian market first — diverse faces, local settings (university halls, open offices, co-working spaces in Algiers, Annaba, Oran). Avoid imagery that looks exclusively Western or generic global stock.
Natural, bright lighting Prefer natural daylight or soft studio light. Images should feel open and optimistic — not dark, moody, or artificially dramatic. Avoid heavy filters, vignettes, and high-contrast edits.
Action-oriented framing Frame subjects in the middle of doing something — typing, presenting, reviewing a contract, receiving feedback. Avoid empty, static portraits with no context. The brand is about momentum and progress.
Clean, uncluttered backgrounds Backgrounds should be simple — a desk, a wall, a campus corridor. Avoid very busy scenes that compete with the subject. When placing text over images, always add a dark overlay first.
Emotion and aspiration The best imagery for Bridge Up captures a feeling — the relief of landing a first gig, the confidence of a recruiter finding the right fit, the pride of a student earning their first certificate. Lead with emotion.

Tone of Voice

How Bridge Up speaks — across all three audiences, every touchpoint, every word.

01 — Brand Voice Pillars

Every word written under the Bridge Up brand — whether a hero headline, a toast notification, or a contract reminder — must reflect these four voice pillars.

Empowering not patronising Give people confidence to act. Never talk down to students or treat employers as superior.
Direct not vague Say exactly what you mean. No filler. No corporate jargon. One clear action per message.
Human not robotic Write like a person, not a system. Warm, real, and conversational — even in error messages.
Optimistic not naive Lead with possibility and forward motion. Acknowledge difficulty, but always point toward progress.

05 — Micro-copy Rules

Micro-copy covers every small text element in the UI — tooltips, placeholders, toasts, labels, confirmations. These rules apply to all three audiences.

Element Rule Example
Placeholder text Use instructional phrases, not labels. Describe the expected input naturally. "e.g. Web development, Marketing, Design"
Success toast Confirm what happened and hint at what comes next. Keep under 10 words. ✓ Contract signed. We've notified the employer.
Error toast Never blame the user. Say what went wrong and what to do next. Couldn't save changes — check your connection and try again.
Warning Be specific about the risk and the action needed. Never just say "Warning." ⚠ Your profile is 60% complete — employers can't find you yet.
Empty state Acknowledge the state, then give one clear action to fix it. Never a dead end. No contracts yet. Post your first role to start engaging talent.
Button labels Use verb + noun. Never "Click here", "Submit", or "OK" alone. Post a role · Sign the contract · Claim certificate
Confirmation modal The destructive action button must state the action, not just "Confirm" or "Yes." Delete this role · Cancel
Onboarding steps Frame each step as a benefit, not a task. Lead with the outcome. "Tell us your field — so we only show you relevant opportunities."

06 — Universal Grammar Rules

Always

Use active voice — "We matched you with 3 employers" not "You have been matched"
Write sentence case for everything except the logo — "Post a role", not "Post A Role"
Use the Oxford comma in lists — "students, employers, and universities"
Use numerals for numbers — "3 candidates", not "three candidates"
Refer to the platform as "Bridge Up" — never "the app", "the site", or "BU"
Lead headlines with a verb when writing to students — "Earn", "Start", "Unlock"

Never

Never use exclamation marks in error or warning messages — they feel alarming
Never use ALL CAPS for emphasis in body copy — use bold instead
Never refer to students as "users", "applicants", or "resources"
Never refer to employers as "clients" — they are partners on the platform
Never end a sentence with a preposition in formal (university) copy
Never mix languages mid-sentence in the UI — Arabic and French versions should be fully separate
